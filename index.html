robotics gallery

name :

robotic gallery.

area :

the gallery spans over 11,000 square meters, making it one of the largest attractions in the robotics gallery.


Interactive Exhabits :

the gallery hosted over 200 robots forms 79 different types, allowing visitors to explore various applications of robotic in fields like medicine agriculture, and defense.

Features :


Reception Robots:

Visitors are greeted by humanoid robots that can interact socially, welcoming them and providing introductions to the gallery.


VR Zone:

A dedicated area for virtual and augmented reality experiences, allowing guests to engage with robotics in a digital environment.


Robo Café:

Visitors can enjoy food prepared by robotic chefs and served by robot waiters, combining entertainment with dining.


Learning Experience:

Each section of the gallery is designed to educate visitors about the utility and innovations in robotics, enhancing public understanding of this field. The layout includes a central atrium and various thematic displays.


Family-Friendly:

The Robotics Gallery is designed for visitors of all ages, making it an ideal educational outing for families. It aims to foster a scientific temper encourage learning through entertainment.


Themes and Zones:

The gallery is divided into different zones that highlight specific aspects of robotics, such as space robotics, underwater robots, and biomimicry robots. Each zone focuses on how robots are applied in these fields.


Design and Integration:

The Robotics Gallery is designed to blend with the natural and architectural elements of the Science City. The building's architecture draws inspiration from the mechanics of robotics, creating an environment that feels futuristic and technologically advanced.

Aquarium

name :

Aquarium.


area :

the aquarium is the largest public aquarium in india spread over 11,500 square meters..


Diverse Marine Species:

The gallery houses 11,600 aquatic animals from around 188 different species, including reef sharks, zebra sharks, alligator gar, black pacu fish, and more. It offers an underwater walkway tunnel where visitors can observe marine life in their natural environment.


Shark Tunnel:

A key highlight is the 28-meter shark tunnel where visitors can walk through an acrylic tunnel surrounded by a variety of sharks and other marine creatures. This feature provides an exhilarating view of sea life.



5D Theatre:

In addition to live exhibits, the Aquatic Gallery offers a 5D theatre experience, allowing visitors to dive deeper into the underwater world through immersive shows.


Interactive Touchpad Displays:

Visitors can engage with touchpad displays that provide detailed information about the different species present in the gallery. QR code-based learning is also integrated into the exhibits, making it both educational and entertaining.


Marine Tanks:

The gallery features 68 tanks, each dedicated to different marine habitats and species, ranging from tropical fish to deep-sea creatures.



Bioluminescent Tanks:

Some tanks are designed to showcase bioluminescent species, which glow in the dark. This provides a mesmerizing display of marine life that naturally produces light, such as jellyfish and certain types of fish.
